# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Agency operators and multi-brand managers reviewing analytics across multiple YouTube channels and web stores. They use this in a professional context — likely on a desktop or large monitor, often alongside other tools. Their primary job is pattern recognition: spotting revenue trends, comparing channel performance, catching anomalies before they become problems. They're comfortable with dense information and expect the tool to respect their time.

## Product Purpose

A unified analytics dashboard that surfaces YouTube and web store metrics in one place, with filtering by channel and store. It exists because creators and agencies managing multiple revenue streams shouldn't need to context-switch between platforms to understand overall performance. Success looks like: open the dashboard, understand what's happening in 30 seconds, know what to act on.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, dense. Linear / Raycast energy — quiet confidence, data forward, nothing decorative. The interface disappears behind the data.

## Anti-references

- **Neon-on-dark gamer dashboards** (Grafana defaults, purple neon, unrestrained dark-mode maximalism)
- **Generic SaaS cream** (Intercom / HubSpot — warm whites, rounded everything, friendly but forgettable)
- **Google Analytics / Data Studio** (bureaucratic, over-charted, information overload without hierarchy)
- **Canva-adjacent colorfulness** (too many colors, gradient buttons, illustrative icons, visual noise)

## Design Principles

1. **Data first, decoration last.** Every element earns its place by carrying information. If removing it doesn't break comprehension, remove it.
2. **Signal through restraint.** The three accent colors (amber, teal, purple) exist to encode meaning — YouTube, Store, Combined. Using them decoratively dilutes the system.
3. **Dense without overwhelming.** Agency users scan many metrics quickly. Hierarchy comes from scale, weight, and rhythm — not from visual noise or additional chrome.
4. **Quiet confidence.** If a user notices the interface more than their data, something is wrong. The design succeeds when it becomes invisible.
5. **Precision as personality.** Monospace values, tight spacing, semantic color — these are the character of the product. Don't sand them down for softness.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Keyboard navigability, screen reader support for key metrics. Color is used as a redundant signal alongside labels and icons — never as the sole differentiator.
